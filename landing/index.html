How it works

Three steps. About two seconds.

No windows to open, nothing to copy-paste. Scriva lives in your menu bar and works wherever your cursor is.

  1. 01

    Hold your hotkey and speak

    Press and hold from any app. Talk like you talk — pauses, “ums” and all.

  2. 02

    Scriva transcribes and polishes

    Your speech becomes text, then gets cleaned up: filler removed, punctuation fixed, formatting sorted.

  3. 03

    Clean text appears at your cursor

    Release the key and finished text lands right where you were typing. Email, Slack, your editor — anywhere.

Features

Everything you need. Nothing watching you.

Scriva does one job extremely well, and it does it on your terms.

Works in every app

Scriva types at your cursor, system-wide. If you can type there, you can talk there.

Transcribe + polish

Not a raw dump. Filler words gone, punctuation fixed, formatting cleaned — finished text, first try.

Bring your own key

Transcribe with Groq or OpenAI. Polish with Claude, OpenAI, or Gemini. Your keys, your choice, your bill — usually pennies.

Privacy-first

No telemetry, no accounts, no middleman. Your audio and text go to the provider you chose — and nowhere else.

Fast

Built for low latency, end to end. Release the key and your text appears almost instantly.

Open source & free

MIT licensed. Read the code, audit it, fork it, make it yours. No trial, no tier, no catch.

Use the models you want

Scriva is model-agnostic. Plug in the providers you already trust — and switch anytime.

  • Groq
  • OpenAI
  • Anthropic Claude
  • Google Gemini

Transcription runs on Groq or OpenAI. Cleanup can use Claude, OpenAI, or Gemini — or be turned off entirely if you want the raw transcript.

Yours, all the way down

Software that answers to you, not a subscription.

There’s no Scriva backend. Nothing phones home. Your audio and text travel one route: straight from your Mac to the provider you picked, using your own key. Don’t take our word for it — the code is public.

  • No subscription. Free, forever. You only pay your provider’s per-use rates.
  • No telemetry. No analytics, no crash reporters, no “anonymous usage data.”
  • Your keys, your data. Keys stay on your machine. Audio goes only to your chosen provider.
  • MIT licensed. Use it, ship it, fork it — commercially, personally, however.
  • Auditable. Every line is on GitHub for anyone to read.
  • No account. Download, paste a key, start talking. That's the whole setup.

FAQ

Questions, answered

Yes. Scriva is MIT-licensed open source — no trial, no pro tier, no locked features. The only cost is what your AI provider charges for the requests you make with your own key, which for typical dictation is a matter of cents per month.

Scriva doesn’t run its own servers — it sends your audio directly to an AI provider using an API key you supply. You’ll need at least one key (Groq or OpenAI) for transcription; Groq offers free keys and takes about two minutes to set up. Paste it into Scriva’s settings once and you’re done.

macOS today. Windows and Linux are planned — the core is built to be portable. Watch the GitHub repo for progress.

Not by Scriva. Audio is captured, sent once to the provider you chose for transcription, and discarded. There’s no Scriva backend, no logging of audio or transcripts, and no telemetry. Your relationship with the AI provider is governed by their terms — which you picked, with your own account.

After transcription, an optional cleanup pass removes filler words (“um”, “uh”, false starts), fixes punctuation and capitalization, and tidies formatting — while keeping your words and meaning intact. It runs on Claude, OpenAI, or Gemini with your key, and you can turn it off for raw transcripts.
